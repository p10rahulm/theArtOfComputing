The Art of Computing
Generously Supported by a Walmart CSR grant.
February 17th and 18th, 2024. IISc Bangalore.
Welcome to the "Art of Computing" wworkshop, a multidisciplinary symposium dedicated to exploring the intersections of computing with various scientific and artistic disciplines. Scheduled for February 17-18, this event assembles esteemed speakers from prominent institutions such as the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), and Brown University.
Our distinguished panel, featuring Prof. Y. Narahari, Prof. C Pandurangan (IISc), Prof. Shriram Krishnamurthi (Brown University, USA), Appupen (George Mathen), Prof. Jaikumar Radhakrishnan (TIFR and ICTS Bangalore) among others, will present on a range of topics. These include Algorithmic Agriculture, the intricacies of Deep Learning, the integration of AI in graphic novels, and the mathematical principles underlying juggling. The workshop aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the role of computing in diverse fields, from agriculture to the arts.
Organized into thematic sessions such as Artificial Intelligence, "Math, Movement, Puzzles, and Paradoxes", Computational Thinking, and a fun science communication Workshop, the workshop is structured to cater to a wide range of academic interests. In addition to the lectures, the conference will host a workshop by Argha Manna titled "Drawing in the Science Classroom." This session will investigate the use of comics as an educational tool in Computer Science and other scientific areas.
This workshop is a gathering for scholars, practitioners, educators, and students in the field of computer science and related disciplines. It aims to foster a deeper understanding of the multifaceted nature of computing and its significant impact across various sectors. We invite you to join us in this academic endeavor that promises insightful discussions and innovative perspectives on the convergence of technology, science, and art.

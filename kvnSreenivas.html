Stop Worrying and Start Sampling: The Power of Randomness in Computation.
Abstract: If we worry less about what happens in the worst-case scenario and shift our focus to more plausible scenarios, we can achieve a lot of things. The focus of this talk is to demonstrate how randomness helps in designing very fast, simple, and elegant algorithms that only have a little chance of failing. These techniques find many applications, ranging from calculating pi to rendering video games.
Speaker Bio
K. V. N. Sreenivas is a PhD student at the Indian Institute of Science Bengaluru, advised by Dr. Arindam Khan. He did his BTech in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT Bombay and MTech (Research) from IISc. He is a recipient of the Google PhD Fellowship and Recipient of Dr. MNS Swamy Medal for Best MTech (Research) Thesis. His research interests mainly lie in Approximation and Online algorithms, and Beyond worst-case models.
